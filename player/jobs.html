Jobs & Professions

The working and currently configured ways a character gathers materials, earns money, produces goods, treats players, or develops a reputation in RDR2PF.

How Jobs Connect to the Server

RDR2PF does not treat every activity the same way. Some systems are formal jobs with job names and ranks, while others are open-world professions that any character can perform. Living Mastery records repeated behavior separately from the VORP job field.

Hunting & Butcher

The hunting resource can grant configured animal products when animals are skinned, plucked, picked up, or sold. The butcher system is enabled and its job lock is currently disabled, so butcher NPCs are not restricted to characters holding the butcher job.

Configured butcher locations include Valentine, Strawberry, Blackwater, Annesburg, Van Horn, Rhodes, Armadillo, Tumbleweed, Landing, and Saint Denis.

Every givenItem entry must exist in MariaDB. Current examples include venison, pork, porkfat, biggame, pelts, antlers, claws, teeth, feathers, and animal-specific carcass items.

Known limitation

Some Bison, Whitetail Buck, and Wild Turkey interactions have been inconsistent even though the animal hashes are configured. Keep those cases marked as investigating until they are retested successfully.

Fishing

The fishing resource is enabled. Fish, bait, and lure rewards are inventory items, so their exact IDs must exist in items.item. Confirmed database examples include fish IDs such as a_c_fishbluegil_01_ms, a_c_fishperch_01_sm, and a_c_fishsalmonsockeye_01_ms, plus bait or lure IDs such as p_baitBread01x, p_baitCheese01x, p_baitCorn01x, p_baitWorm01x, and p_FinisdFishlure01x.

Do not replace these internal IDs with their display names.

Crafting & Cooking

Crafting is the conversion layer between job materials and usable goods. Recipes can require ingredients, produce one or more rewards, belong to a category, use a location restriction, require a job, or charge currency.

The current breakfast investigation confirms that consumable_breakfast, egg, eggs, and pork all exist as database entries. That does not by itself prove the recipe is correct because egg and eggs are separate internal items.

Use the Item & Crafting Validation page before changing any recipe.

Lumberjack

The lumberjack configuration requires the inventory item hatchet. This corrects the older wiki note that identified lumberaxe as the required tool.

Tree chopping is blocked in Annesburg, Armadillo, Blackwater, Lagras, Rhodes, Saint Denis, Strawberry, Tumbleweed, Valentine, and Van Horn. The configured cooldown is 60 seconds.

Mining

The mining configuration requires pickaxe. Annesburg is currently the only listed town with mining allowed; Armadillo, Blackwater, Lagras, Rhodes, Saint Denis, Strawberry, Tumbleweed, Valentine, and Van Horn are restricted.

Configured rewards include clay, coal, copper, iron, nitrite, rock, and salt.

Database mismatch found

The mining configuration references goldnugget, but the current MariaDB registry contains gold_nugget. Do not silently change it in the wiki or resource until the mining repair is approved and tested. This is a confirmed example of why the item validator is necessary.

Doctor / Medic

The formal job name is doctor. Rank 0 is Doctor and rank 1 is Nurse. Configured doctor stations and shared medical storage exist in Valentine, Strawberry, and Saint Denis.

Configured commands include /calldoctor, /cancelDoctorAlert, /finishDoctorAlert, and /doctormenu.

Living Mastery Connections

Formal jobs and open-world professions are not the same thing as Living Mastery. The implemented progression system can record repeated behavior and calculate titles independently of a character's VORP job.
